BikeRoar Press: A Tribute to Jens Voigt

        
By  Craig Cahill
        
        · Friday, Oct 31, 2014
      







    

    




    
Jens Voigt is a living cycling legend. Few riders before him have captured the hearts and minds of the fans like the “Shut Up Legs!” man from Hamburg, Germany. Not since Eddy Merckx — argued, the greatest cyclist ever — has a single rider stood as the pedestal of all cycling fans. Now, at age 43, and with the 2014 road calendar closing in, so too is the 17 year career of the skinny, troubled schoolboy who had too much energy to burn.


    



    

      
People acknowledge that I'm a really hard working person, and every now and then I get rewarded with a nice win, but sometimes I also fail, and I explode into a million pieces, but I come back next day again you know.

      
    


    



    
Turning professional in 1997, Jens’ cycling career began with the Australian-German connection of Heiko Salzwedel and the Australian Institute of Sport, before returning to European soil and France’s famous GAN team (later known as Crédit Agricole). Six successful years and 20 victories later — including Tour de France stage wins, time in the coveted Maillot Jaune, and a pivotal role in Germany’s 2000 Olympic glory — the signature of Jens Voigt was top priority for every professional cycling team. In 2004, after inking a deal with Team CSC and Bjarne Riis, Jens found a home he would make his own for another 6 successful years.


    



    

      
Fortunately in human nature, you blend out the bad things and you only remember the good things. So I have a lot of good memories about it and no regrets.

      
    


    



    
In 2011 a new cycling super team was born. Jens Voigt headlined Team Leopard Trek alongside Frank Schleck, Andy Schleck, and Fabian Cancellara, where he selflessly rode out his days more for others’ glory than his own. Such selflessness is a hallmark of Jens’ career and a tribute to his selection in 17 Grande Boucle starts. So respected is he by fans, riders and race promoters alike that the Tour de France peloton officially farewell him in 2014 by letting him ride afront the bunch for the entire first lap of the famous Champs-Élysées.


    



    

      
It's good that it ends... many hard years... the body and the head they... not really into it anymore. They're telling me "Ok, keep it up, one more year for you and then, it's finished". And now I've reached a moment where I don't have to say ShutUpLegs any more.

      
    


    



    
Beside the greatest names in cycling, the palmares of Jens Voigt pales in size and stature; 19 race victories including 4 Grand Tour stages. Compare that to Eddy Merckx; 525 race victories including 64 Grand Tour stages and 3 World Championships. But numbers alone only ever count in record books, and the name “Jens Voigt” is published in a place of more prestige…  cycling folk-law… where his “Shut Up Legs!” motto will live forever, and his harder-than-cement racing style admired always.


    
His palmares make him great, but his character, panache, and personality make him the greatest we’ve seen in 50 years or more. Thank you Jens, for everything.


    



    
    








  

    

      
        

          
          Author: Craig Cahill
